Okay, backups are done. BUT….this doesn’t really protect you from HACKERS trying to steal the data in the first place. Think about it. You have a backup when they do steal it or encrypt it and ask for some bitcoin to get it back. So, how can you make it harder for them to grab in the first place?

There is so much you can do right now to protect yourself but my goal is to boil it down to the bare minimum. What actions get you the most bang for your buck?

Well, it’s three actions. Sorry.

First, you need to find all the devices that are attached to your network. I don’t want to get too technical so just walk around your business and locate all of them. Make a map if you want to feel like an explorer.

Once you know what you have, now you need to make sure the software that runs on these devices is the BARE MINIMUM needed to do the job on that device. For example, do you have a desktop that you use as your cash system? Fine. But you should remove the internet browser, minesweeper and anything else that’s on it. Yes, this won’t make you the most popular boss but while you are deleting all those apps think of all the sleepless nights you will be saving yourself.

The third and last action is to make sure that your operating system and all those NECESSARY applications are patched and up to date.

Keeping your software up to date is another ritual you need to perform. So, just add this to the celebration that happens when you back up you data. Call it PATCH AND BACK or whatever.
